# Effective RTI Strategies — Insider Knowledge

*Lessons from thousands of successful RTI filings*

---

## The Golden Rules of RTI Success

### Rule 1: Ask for Documents, Not Explanations

❌ Bad: "Why is my pension claim delayed?"
✅ Good: "Provide the current status of pension claim bearing PPO number [X], the name and designation of the officer presently handling it, and copies of all notings/decisions made on the file since [date]."

Asking "why" invites an excuse. Asking for documents forces transparency.

### Rule 2: One Application, One Subject

Filing one RTI that asks about 10 different issues across 3 departments is asking for partial rejection and confusion. File separate RTIs for each distinct matter. The ₹10 fee per application is worth it.

### Rule 3: Reference Numbers Are Gold

Always include any reference/registration/acknowledgment numbers from your previous dealings with the department. This anchors your RTI to a specific file they cannot claim doesn't exist.

### Rule 4: Ask for "All Notings on the File"

The most powerful question in any RTI: *"Provide certified copies of all notings, orders, and correspondence on [file/application number/matter] from [date] to present."*

File notings reveal the internal decision-making trail — why things were approved, rejected, delayed, and who decided what.

### Rule 5: Ask for the Officer's Name

"State the name, designation, and employee ID of the officer currently responsible for processing [your application/matter]" creates accountability. Officers are far more diligent when their names are attached to delays.

### Rule 6: The Section 4 Trap

If you need information that should already be publicly available (department budget, staff roster, service delivery norms), ask: *"Provide the information published under Section 4(1)(b) of the RTI Act regarding [specific information]."*

This puts the CPIO on notice that you know this information should already be proactively published.

### Rule 7: Counter Section 8 Rejections Strategically

If your RTI is rejected citing Section 8(1)(j) (personal information), ask: *"The information I seek relates to actions taken by public servants in their official capacity, which constitute public activity under Section 8(1)(j) and cannot be exempted. Please reconsider."*

The Supreme Court in Girish Ramchandra Deshpande v. CIC ruled that the performance of public duties by a public servant is not personal information.

## Domain-Specific Strategies

### For Pension / Retirement Benefit RTIs

Ask for:
1. Current status of the claim with stage-wise details
2. Name of officer processing it and their contact
3. Copy of all documents received and pending
4. The specific rule/order under which any deduction or delay is justified
5. Whether any sanction order has been issued — if yes, copy; if not, reason for delay

### For Job / Recruitment RTIs

Powerful questions:
1. "Provide the list of candidates shortlisted at each stage of selection for [post], including their marks/scores in each stage"
2. "Provide the composition of the selection committee including their names and designations"
3. "Provide the marks/scores obtained by Roll No. [your roll number] in each stage, and the cut-off marks for each category at each stage"

Note: Supreme Court (UPSC v. R.K. Jain, 2018) — answer scripts are not personal information and must be provided.

### For Land / Property RTIs

Ask for:
1. Status of land records / mutation proceedings
2. Copies of relevant khasra/jamabandi/patta documents
3. Name of officials who processed mutations and on what dates
4. Whether any stay order / court order affects the matter

### For Municipal Service RTIs

Ask for:
1. Number of complaints received about [specific issue — roads, water, garbage] in your ward in the last 6 months
2. Number resolved vs pending
3. The Service Level Agreement (SLA) timelines for resolution
4. Copy of any correspondence / work orders related to your specific complaint number

### For RTI on RTI Delays (Meta-RTI)

If your RTI itself is delayed — file an RTI asking:
*"Provide details of all RTI applications received in your department in the last 6 months, the number responded to within 30 days vs beyond, and the action taken against PIOs who delayed."*

This creates pressure within the department.

## Tactics When Information Is Refused

### Tactic 1: Reframe, Don't Refile

If CPIO refuses citing Section 8 exemption, don't just refile the same question. In the First Appeal, specifically argue:
- The specific exemption claimed does not apply to this information because [reason]
- Public interest override under Section 8(2) justifies disclosure
- The information is 20+ years old, covered by Section 8(3) sunset

### Tactic 2: The Narrower Ask

If CPIO claims your request is too broad ("voluminous"), file a new RTI with narrower, specific questions targeting only the most critical piece of information you need.

### Tactic 3: The Parallel Track

While your RTI appeal is pending, simultaneously:
- File a formal written complaint with the department head
- File a CPGRAMS grievance at pgportal.gov.in
- For financial matters, contact the Parliamentary Constituency MP office

This multi-pronged approach often resolves issues faster than RTI alone.

### Tactic 4: The Public Interest Argument

For any refusal under discretionary exemptions (Section 8(1)(d), (e), (j)), always argue:
*"The larger public interest in disclosure significantly outweighs any harm — providing this information would promote transparency and prevent future similar grievances affecting thousands of citizens."*

This invokes Section 8(2) and forces the CPIO to explicitly justify overriding public interest.

## Appeals — What Works

### For First Appeals

- Keep it formal, factual, brief (1-2 pages)
- Cite the exact section of RTI Act that the CPIO violated
- Include your calculation of days elapsed since filing
- Attach: copy of original RTI, proof of dispatch, CPIO's response (if any)
- Request a specific remedy (not just "provide information" but "provide within 7 working days")

### For Second Appeals (CIC/SIC)

- File online at cic.gov.in for Central matters
- Clearly state: all prior steps taken, dates, and outcomes
- CIC has powers to impose ₹25,000 penalty — mention this is what you are requesting if delay was malicious
- CIC decisions are public — this creates reputational pressure on departments

## RTI for Vulnerable Situations

### If You Are BPL (Below Poverty Line)
- Zero application fee — attach BPL card copy
- Mention BPL status prominently in application body
- CPIO cannot charge you anything — if they try, it is grounds for a penalty complaint

### Emergency / Life-Liberty Situations
- Mark application clearly: "LIFE AND LIBERTY MATTER — 48 HOUR RESPONSE REQUIRED"
- Cite Section 7(1) proviso explicitly
- Examples: denial of hospital treatment, illegal detention, ration denial causing starvation

### Senior Citizens / Persons with Disabilities
- No special fee exemption (only BPL qualifies), but you may request information in accessible format
- Can nominate a family member to submit/collect on your behalf — include an authorization letter

## After Filing — What to Do

| Day | Action |
|-----|--------|
| Day 0 | File RTI. Keep acknowledgment/receipt. |
| Day 1-29 | Wait for response. |
| Day 30 | If no response — immediately prepare First Appeal. |
| Day 31+ | File First Appeal. Don't delay — you have 30 days from Day 30. |
| Appeal Day 1-30 | Wait for FAA decision. |
| Appeal Day 31 | If no FAA decision — prepare Second Appeal to Information Commission. |
| At any point | If you receive satisfactory information — update your status diary. Mark RESOLVED. |
