# RTI Act 2005 — Key Sections Reference

## Section 2: Definitions

**Section 2(h) — Public Authority**: Any authority or body established by the Constitution, any law made by Parliament or State Legislature, or by notification by the Central/State Government. Includes:
- All government ministries and departments
- Government companies and corporations
- Bodies substantially financed by government
- NGOs substantially financed by government

**Section 2(f) — Information**: Any material in any form, including records, documents, memos, emails, opinions, advices, press releases, circulars, orders, logbooks, contracts, reports, papers, samples, models, data material held in any electronic form.

**Section 2(j) — Right to Information**: Right to inspect works, documents, records; take notes, extracts, or certified copies; take certified samples; obtain information in diskette/floppy/tape/video cassette or any other electronic mode.

---

## Section 3: Citizens' Right

Every citizen shall have the right to information. Only citizens can file RTI — not companies, trusts, or non-citizens.

---

## Section 4: Proactive Disclosure (Obligation to Publish)

Every public authority must proactively publish:
- Organisation, function, duties, powers
- Rules, regulations, instructions, manuals
- Details of officials
- Budget allocated and expenditure
- Subsidy programmes, beneficiaries, criteria

**Practical use**: If information is already published under Section 4, it can be accessed without an RTI.

---

## Section 6: Request for Obtaining Information

**Section 6(1)**: A citizen desiring information shall make a request in writing (or electronically) to the CPIO specifying the information sought, along with the prescribed fee.

**Section 6(2)**: The applicant is NOT required to give reasons for seeking information or any personal details beyond what is necessary for contacting them.

**Section 6(3)**: If the information is held by another public authority, the CPIO receiving the application must transfer it within 5 days and inform the applicant.

---

## Section 7: Disposal of Request

**Section 7(1)**: Information must be provided within **30 days** of receipt.

**Proviso to Section 7(1)**: Where information sought **concerns the life or liberty of a person**, it must be provided within **48 hours**.

**Section 7(2)**: If the CPIO fails to respond within 30 days, it is deemed a **refusal** and the applicant may file a First Appeal.

**Section 7(5)**: Persons below the poverty line are **exempt from fees**.

**Section 7(6)**: If information is not provided within the time limit, it must be provided **free of charge**.

**Section 7(8)**: The CPIO must inform the applicant of reasons for rejection, the appeal period, and particulars of the Appellate Authority.

---

## Section 8: Exemptions from Disclosure

Information that cannot be disclosed includes:
- 8(1)(a): National security, sovereignty
- 8(1)(d): Commercial confidence, trade secrets
- 8(1)(i): Cabinet papers
- 8(1)(j): Personal information with no relationship to public activity

**Critical**: Section 8(2) — Exemptions can be overridden if **public interest in disclosure outweighs the harm**.

---

## Section 19: Appeals

**Section 19(1) — First Appeal**:
- Within **30 days** of CPIO's decision or response due date
- To the First Appellate Authority (officer senior to CPIO)
- Authority must decide within **30 days** (extendable by 15 days)

**Section 19(3) — Second Appeal**:
- Within **90 days** of First Appellate Authority's decision
- To the Central/State Information Commission

**Section 19(5)**: Burden of proof is on the CPIO to justify denial.

---

## Section 20: Penalties

**Section 20(1)**: CIC/SIC SHALL impose penalty of **Rs 250 per day** up to maximum **Rs 25,000** for:
- Refusing to receive an application
- Not furnishing information within time
- Malafidely denying information
- Knowingly giving incorrect/misleading information
- Destroying information subject to a request

---

## Key Drafting Tips

1. Never mention your reasons for filing — you are not obligated to explain
2. Be specific — ask for named documents, records, dates, officer names
3. Include reference/file/claim numbers in the application
4. Count days precisely for appeal deadlines
5. One RTI per authority — file separate applications for different departments
6. Fee: Rs 10 for Central Govt; Rs 10-50 for states
7. Language: English or official language of the area
