# Common RTI Rejection Reasons & How to Counter Them

## Top 10 Rejection Reasons and Responses

---

### 1. "Information is exempt under Section 8(1)(j) — personal information"
**When used**: When asking about another government employee's service record, salary, or actions.

**Counter**:
- If the person is a public official and the information relates to their *public duties*, it is NOT personal information.
- CIC has held repeatedly: "Information about public servants performing public duties is not exempt under Section 8(1)(j)."
- Cite: Girish Ramchandra Deshpande vs CIC — SC held that personal information of public employees related to discharge of public duties is disclosable.
- Reframe the request: "Provide the service record of [Designation], not [Name]" — focus on the position, not the person.

---

### 2. "Information not available / record not traceable"
**When used**: Department claims it doesn't have the record.

**Counter**:
- Ask for written confirmation that the record doesn't exist — this itself is information.
- Ask for the register/index showing what files exist on the subject.
- In appeal, cite Section 4(1)(b) — every public authority is *required* to maintain certain records. If they don't, that itself may be a violation.
- Ask: "Provide a certified statement that no records exist relating to [subject] along with the name of the officer who verified this."

---

### 3. "Application fee not paid / wrong payment method"
**When used**: IPO not accepted, online payment not credited, etc.

**Counter**:
- In first appeal, provide proof of payment (receipt, IPO number).
- CIC/SIC rulings have held that procedural defects in fee payment that can be cured must be given an opportunity to cure.
- Re-file with correct payment and calculate the response timeline from re-filing.

---

### 4. "Too broad / vague / voluminous request"
**When used**: Department claims compliance would "disproportionately divert resources."

**Counter**:
- This defense is available under Section 7(9) — but it's heavily overused.
- Narrow your request in the appeal: replace "all documents" with specific named documents.
- CIC has held that PIOs must assist applicants in narrowing requests rather than outright refusing.
- Ask in the appeal: "Provide a list of what documents are held on this subject" — that itself answers the volumetricity claim.

---

### 5. "Information relates to third party — Section 11 procedure invoked"
**When used**: Your question touches on records belonging to a private person or company.

**Counter**:
- Section 11 is a *procedure*, not a refusal. The CPIO must notify the third party and decide within 40 days.
- If they use Section 11 as an outright refusal without following the procedure, appeal on procedural grounds.
- If the third party received government money/contracts/licenses, their information is usually disclosable.

---

### 6. "Information is sub-judice / under investigation"
**When used**: Department claims matter is in court or under investigation.

**Counter**:
- Section 8(1)(h) — applies only to information that would "impede the process of investigation or apprehension or prosecution."
- Past records that led to an investigation are generally not exempt.
- Ask specifically for historical records, not ongoing investigation files.
- CIC has held that "sub-judice" is not a blanket exemption.

---

### 7. "No response within 30 days (deemed refusal)"
**Not a rejection reason — but the most common problem.**

**Action**: Do NOT wait further. File First Appeal under Section 19(1) immediately.
- Note exact number of days elapsed
- State that non-response constitutes "deemed refusal" per Section 7(2)
- Request penalty imposition under Section 20(1)

---

### 8. "Information is held by another department"
**When used**: Valid transfer under Section 6(3), but sometimes used as delay tactic.

**Counter**:
- Transfer must happen within 5 days and you must be informed.
- If not informed, file appeal against the original authority.
- If transferred, ensure the new department's 30-day clock started correctly.
- If both departments claim the other holds the information, file appeal and name both.

---

### 9. "Applicant is not a citizen of India / Not eligible"
**When used**: Rarely, but sometimes when applicant's name sounds non-Indian.

**Counter**:
- Attach a self-declaration of citizenship in the appeal.
- Note that the Act says "citizen" — voter ID, Aadhaar, or passport can prove citizenship.
- CIC has held that the PIO cannot demand citizenship proof unless there is specific reason to doubt.

---

### 10. "Information pertains to Cabinet/Council of Ministers decisions"
**When used**: Section 8(1)(i) — Cabinet deliberations.

**Counter**:
- The exemption covers *deliberations*, not decisions.
- Ask specifically for the *decision* and the *reasons for the decision* — these must be disclosed once the matter is over and the decision is taken.
- Cite Section 8(2) — public interest override.
- Cite Section 8(3) — after 20 years, even exempt information must be disclosed.

---

## Drafting to Avoid Rejections

### Use This Language:
- "Certified copy of..." (not "all documents about...")
- "The name and designation of the officer who..." (not "who is responsible for...")
- "The specific provision of law/rule/circular under which..." (not "why did you do...")
- "Status of [file/application/claim] bearing number [X] as on [date]"
- "Number of complaints received regarding [subject] between [date range]"

### Avoid This Language:
- "All documents related to corruption..."
- "Why has nothing been done..."
- "Explain the reason for the delay..."
- Anything that sounds like a complaint rather than a request for records

### The Anti-Rejection Clause (Always Include):
"If any part of this information is withheld, please cite the specific provision of Section 8 or Section 9 of the RTI Act, 2005 applicable to each withheld item, along with the specific reasons and the period during which such exemption applies."
